# Corpsefag

The **Corpsefag** is a scavenger species within the animals ecosystem. Unlike predators (Snakes) that hunt active prey, or herbivores (Prey/Amphibia) that graze on grass, Corpsefags play a critical cleanup role: they feast on the static corpses of dead snakes left behind on the map.

## Behavior Profile

- **Role**: Scavenger
- **Diet**: Static snake corpses (represented as `HashSet` coordinates on the grid)
- **Movement**: Can navigate most terrain, avoiding obstacles like water, rocks, and living snakes.
- **Goal**: Follow scent trails to locate and consume corpses to score points and stay alive.

## AI & Observation Model

The Corpsefag is driven by a custom PPO (Proximal Policy Optimization) model trained to seek out corpses using a directional "smelling" mechanism.

### Observation Space (18 floats)

Unlike `Prey` which uses an absolute grid, or `Snake` which uses a rotated egocentric grid, the Corpsefag relies on a hybrid observation vector (`CORPSEFAG_OBS_SIZE = 18`) generated by `get_corpsefag_observation`:

1. **Local Grid (9 floats)**: 
   - A 3x3 grid centered directly on the Corpsefag.
   - Used to detect immediate physical obstacles (e.g. walls, water, rocks, or living snakes).
2. **Directional Smell Rays (8 floats)**:
   - The Corpsefag casts 8 "smell" rays outward in the cardinal and ordinal directions (N, NE, E, SE, S, SW, W, NW).
   - If a corpse falls within the `SMELL_RANGE` (133 cells), the ray corresponding to its angle registers a proximity signal.
   - The signal strength is linearly scaled: `1.0 - (distance / 133.0)`. A value closer to `1.0` means the corpse is right next to the scavenger, while `0.0` means nothing was detected in that direction.
   - This provides dense, continuous tracking information that guides the AI toward food from far across the map.
3. **Score (1 float)**:
   - A normalized scalar (`points / 3.0`) representing the current score/satiation of the Corpsefag.

### Action Space (5 discrete actions)

Like other non-snake species in the simulation, the Corpsefag selects from 5 possible moves each tick:
- `0`: Stand still
- `1`: Move Up (North)
- `2`: Move Right (East)
- `3`: Move Down (South)
- `4`: Move Left (West)

## Training Environment

Corpsefags are trained using `learner/src/learner/train_corpsefag.py`. Because they feed on the dead, their training environment (`corpsefag_environment.py`) takes a unique approach: upon every environment `reset()`, it calls `GameState::spawn_corpses()` to scatter dozens of static corpses across the arena. Every time it eats a piece of a snake's corpse, it gains 1 point. When a Corpsefag accumulates **3 points**, it consumes them all and spawns an egg on its current cell. The egg is visually distinct and will eventually hatch into a new Corpsefag, carrying over its parent's family ID. This guarantees a dense reward signal during PPO rollouts, teaching the model to trust its smell rays and actively pursue the scent of decay.
