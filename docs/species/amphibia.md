# Amphibia

The **Amphibia** is a water-favoring herbivore species within the animals ecosystem. Structurally, it is nearly identical to the standard Prey, but its unique movement capabilities fundamentally alter its survival strategy against predators.

## Behavior Profile

- **Role**: Water-favoring Herbivore
- **Diet**: Grass. Grazing depletes the cell's grass health, which slowly regrows over time.
- **Movement**: Highly capable in Water, moving much faster through aquatic terrain than standard Prey or Snakes. Conversely, it is slightly slower on dry land compared to standard Prey.
- **Goal**: Evade predators (often by utilizing bodies of water for escapes), seek out fresh grass to graze on, and eat enough grass to reproduce.

## AI & Observation Model

The Amphibia is driven by a custom PPO (Proximal Policy Optimization) model, trained entirely independently from the Prey model so it can discover water-specific survival strategies.

### Observation Space (132 floats)

The Amphibia's observation vector (`PREY_OBS_SIZE = 132`) is generated by `get_prey_observation` and shares the exact same layout as the standard Prey. However, the *values* it receives in the terrain grid are different because they are filtered through its unique species profile:

1. **Entity & Terrain Grid (64 floats)**: 
   - An 8x8 absolute grid (up is north).
   - Passable terrain is scaled by `Species::Amphibia.speed_on(terrain) * 0.5`. Unlike standard Prey, water cells read as highly traversable (~0.5) to an Amphibia, teaching the AI that water is a safe and fast escape route.
2. **Global Threat Vector (3 floats)**:
   - `[64]`/`[65]`: Unit direction (east/north) to the nearest alive snake head.
   - `[66]`: Distance to that snake head, normalized as `min(dist / 150, 1)`.
3. **Reproduction Progress (1 float)**:
   - `[67]`: A scalar from `0.0` to `1.0` representing `grass_eaten / PREY_REPRODUCTION_GRASS`.
4. **Grass Health Grid (64 floats)**:
   - `[68..132)`: An 8x8 absolute grid showing grass health (0.0 to 1.0) to locate food.

### Action Space (5 discrete actions)

Amphibias select from the standard 5 grid-movement actions each tick:
- `0`: Stand still
- `1`: Move Up (North)
- `2`: Move Right (East)
- `3`: Move Down (South)
- `4`: Move Left (West)

## Reproduction (Mitosis)

Like standard Prey, Amphibias reproduce when they consume enough grass (`PREY_REPRODUCTION_GRASS = 50.0`). This yields a `+25.0` terminal reward in training. Reproduction is only permitted if no snake heads are within an 8-cell radius, ensuring newborns aren't immediately spawn-camped.

## Training Environment

Amphibias are trained using `learner/src/learner/train_amphibia.py` and the `RustAmphibiaVecEnv` environment. 

While they use the same environment mechanics as standard Prey (mixed-generation self-play against frozen Snake opponents), their independent neural network allows them to learn divergent behaviors. A well-trained Amphibia model will naturally learn to stick closer to coastlines and dive into the water when a Snake's global threat vector approaches, utilizing its asymmetric speed advantage to survive.
